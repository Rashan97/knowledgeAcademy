Introduction

Business English refers to the language used in professional settings. Mastering business communication is essential for career success. This lesson covers professional emails, reports, presentations, and workplace communication.

Professional Email Writing

Email Structure

  1. Subject line: Clear and specific
  2. Greeting: Professional salutation
  3. Opening: State your purpose
  4. Body: Main message with details
  5. Closing: Call to action or next steps
  6. Sign-off: Professional closing and signature

Sample Professional Email

Subject: Meeting Request - Q4 Budget Review


Dear Mr. Johnson,

I hope this email finds you well.

I am writing to request a meeting to discuss the Q4 budget projections for our department. I would like to review the proposed allocations and address any concerns before the final approval.

Would you be available for a 30-minute meeting next Tuesday, November 15th, at 2:00 PM? Please let me know if this time works for you, or suggest an alternative that fits your schedule.

Thank you for your time and consideration.

Best regards,
Sarah Mitchell
Department Manager
Finance Division

Common Business Phrases

Opening Phrases

  • "I am writing to inquire about..."
  • "I would like to inform you that..."
  • "Further to our conversation..."
  • "With reference to your email..."
  • "Thank you for your email regarding..."

Requesting Information

  • "Could you please provide..."
  • "I would appreciate it if you could..."
  • "Would it be possible to..."
  • "I would like to request..."

Closing Phrases

  • "Please let me know if you need any further information."
  • "I look forward to hearing from you."
  • "Thank you for your attention to this matter."
  • "Please do not hesitate to contact me if you have questions."

Business Reports

Report Structure

  1. Title Page: Report title, author, date
  2. Executive Summary: Brief overview of key points
  3. Table of Contents: Section headings and page numbers
  4. Introduction: Background and purpose
  5. Body: Findings, analysis, data
    • Use headings and subheadings
    • Include charts, graphs, tables
    • Present facts and evidence
  6. Conclusions: Summary of findings
  7. Recommendations: Suggested actions
  8. References: Sources cited
  9. Appendices: Supporting documents

Meeting Language

Starting a Meeting

  • "Let's get started."
  • "Thank you all for coming."
  • "The purpose of this meeting is to..."
  • "Today's agenda includes..."

Contributing to Discussion

  • "I'd like to add that..."
  • "In my opinion..."
  • "May I suggest that..."
  • "Building on what Sarah said..."
  • "I agree with John's point about..."

Clarifying and Asking Questions

  • "Could you clarify what you mean by...?"
  • "I'm not sure I understand..."
  • "Could you elaborate on that?"
  • "What are the implications of...?"

Concluding a Meeting

  • "Let's summarize what we've discussed..."
  • "To recap, we've agreed to..."
  • "The action items are..."
  • "Our next meeting will be..."
  • "Thank you for your time and input."

Presentation Skills

Presentation Structure

Introduction (10%)

  • Greet the audience
  • Introduce yourself
  • State the topic and objectives
  • Outline the structure

Body (80%)

  • Present main points logically
  • Use visual aids effectively
  • Provide examples and data
  • Use transitions between sections

Conclusion (10%)

  • Summarize key points
  • Restate main message
  • Call to action
  • Invite questions

Useful Presentation Phrases

Opening:

  • "Good morning, everyone. My name is..."
  • "Today I'm going to talk about..."
  • "My presentation is divided into three parts..."

Transitions:

  • "Moving on to the next point..."
  • "Now let's look at..."
  • "This brings us to..."

Closing:

  • "In conclusion..."
  • "To sum up..."
  • "Thank you for your attention. Are there any questions?"

Negotiation Language

Making Proposals

  • "How about if we..."
  • "Would you consider..."
  • "What if we were to..."
  • "I propose that we..."

Expressing Disagreement Politely

  • "I see your point, however..."
  • "I understand, but have you considered..."
  • "That's interesting, though I think..."
  • "I respectfully disagree because..."

Reaching Agreement

  • "That sounds reasonable."
  • "I think we can agree on that."
  • "Let's move forward with this plan."
  • "We have a deal."

Networking and Small Talk

Starting Conversations

  • "Hi, I'm Sarah. Nice to meet you."
  • "What brings you to this event?"
  • "What do you do?"
  • "How long have you been in this industry?"

Safe Small Talk Topics

  • Current events (non-controversial)
  • Weather
  • Travel
  • Professional interests
  • Industry trends

Key Takeaways

  • Professional emails follow a clear structure and formal tone
  • Business reports require organization and supporting evidence
  • Effective presentations have clear openings, bodies, and conclusions
  • Meeting language helps facilitate productive discussions
  • Polite negotiation language maintains positive relationships
  • Business vocabulary enhances professional communication
