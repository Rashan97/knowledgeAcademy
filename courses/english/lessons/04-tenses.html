Introduction

Verb tenses indicate when an action occurs: past, present, or future. English has 12 main tenses, created by combining three time frames with four aspects: simple, progressive (continuous), perfect, and perfect progressive.

The 12 Verb Tenses

PRESENT TENSES

1. Simple Present

Form: base verb (+ s/es for third person singular)

Uses: Habitual actions, facts, general truths

Examples:

  • I work from home.
  • She teaches English.
  • The sun rises in the east.

2. Present Progressive (Continuous)

Form: am/is/are + verb-ing

Uses: Actions happening now, temporary situations

Examples:

  • I am studying right now.
  • She is reading a book.
  • They are working on a project this week.

3. Present Perfect

Form: have/has + past participle

Uses: Actions completed at an unspecified time, experiences, continuing situations

Examples:

  • I have visited Paris three times.
  • She has finished her homework.
  • They have lived here for five years.

4. Present Perfect Progressive

Form: have/has + been + verb-ing

Uses: Actions that started in the past and continue to the present

Examples:

  • I have been waiting for an hour.
  • She has been studying all day.
  • They have been working since morning.

PAST TENSES

5. Simple Past

Form: verb + ed (or irregular past form)

Uses: Completed actions in the past

Examples:

  • I worked late yesterday.
  • She went to London last year.
  • They finished the project.

6. Past Progressive (Continuous)

Form: was/were + verb-ing

Uses: Actions in progress at a specific time in the past

Examples:

  • I was sleeping when you called.
  • She was studying at 9 PM.
  • They were watching TV all evening.

7. Past Perfect

Form: had + past participle

Uses: Actions completed before another past action

Examples:

  • I had finished dinner before she arrived.
  • She had already left when I got there.
  • They had studied for weeks before the exam.

8. Past Perfect Progressive

Form: had + been + verb-ing

Uses: Ongoing actions that were interrupted or completed in the past

Examples:

  • I had been waiting for two hours when the bus arrived.
  • She had been working there for ten years before she quit.
  • They had been living in Tokyo when the earthquake happened.

FUTURE TENSES

9. Simple Future

Form: will + base verb

Uses: Predictions, spontaneous decisions, future facts

Examples:

  • I will call you tomorrow.
  • She will arrive at 3 PM.
  • It will rain tomorrow.

10. Future Progressive (Continuous)

Form: will + be + verb-ing

Uses: Actions that will be in progress at a specific future time

Examples:

  • I will be working at 5 PM tomorrow.
  • She will be traveling next week.
  • They will be waiting for you at the airport.

11. Future Perfect

Form: will + have + past participle

Uses: Actions that will be completed before a specific future time

Examples:

  • I will have finished by noon.
  • She will have graduated by next year.
  • They will have arrived by the time we get there.

12. Future Perfect Progressive

Form: will + have + been + verb-ing

Uses: Ongoing actions that will continue until a specific future time

Examples:

  • By next month, I will have been working here for five years.
  • She will have been studying for six hours by the time the exam starts.
  • They will have been living in Paris for a decade by 2025.

Key Takeaways

  • English has 12 main tenses: 4 present, 4 past, 4 future
  • Simple tenses express basic actions
  • Progressive (continuous) tenses show ongoing actions
  • Perfect tenses connect two time frames
  • Perfect progressive combines both aspects
  • Regular verbs add -ed for past forms
  • Irregular verbs must be memorized
