Introduction to HVAC

Heating, Ventilation, and Air Conditioning (HVAC) systems maintain comfortable and healthy indoor environments. HVAC design integrates thermodynamics, fluid mechanics, and heat transfer to control temperature, humidity, and air quality efficiently.

Summary

  • Psychrometrics: moist air properties Ï‰ (humidity ratio), Ï† (relative humidity), h (enthalpy), psychrometric chart graphical tool
  • Processes: sensible heating/cooling (constant Ï‰), dehumidification (cooling below T_dp), humidification, mixing (linear combination)
  • Cooling load: transmission (walls/windows), solar gain (SHGC), infiltration, internal (people/lights/equipment), sensible + latent
  • Heating load: transmission and infiltration dominate, design for winter extreme conditions (99% temperature)
  • Refrigeration cycle: evaporator (absorb heat), compressor (work input), condenser (reject heat), expansion valve (throttle)
  • Equipment: split systems (residential), RTU/chillers (commercial), heat pumps (reversible), furnaces/boilers (heating)
  • Ductwork: velocity method or equal friction sizing, round ducts most efficient, seal joints to prevent leakage
  • Efficiency: SEER (seasonal cooling), EER (steady-state), COP (instantaneous), AFUE (furnace), higher values better
  • Standards: ASHRAE 62.1 (ventilation), 90.1 (energy), 55 (comfort), IAQ with filtration MERV 8-16
  • HVAC integrates thermodynamics (cycles), fluid mechanics (airflow), and heat transfer (loads) for comfort and efficiency
