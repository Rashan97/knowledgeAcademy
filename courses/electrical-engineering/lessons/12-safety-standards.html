Grounding and Bonding

Grounding Fundamentals

Grounding vs. Bonding (NEC Article 250): Grounding: Intentional connection to earth Purpose: 1. Limit voltage to ground (lightning, line surges) 2. Stabilize voltage during normal operation 3. Facilitate overcurrent device operation NOT the primary shock protection! Bonding: Connection of conductive parts together Purpose: 1. Establish equipotential (same voltage) 2. Provide low-impedance fault path 3. Ensure fault current sufficient to trip breaker PRIMARY shock protection mechanism! Lockout/Tagout (LOTO)

LOTO Procedures

OSHA 1910.147: The Control of Hazardous Energy Purpose: Prevent unexpected energization during maintenance Protect workers from stored energy release Applies to: Electrical, mechanical, hydraulic, pneumatic, thermal Six Steps of Lockout/Tagout: 1. PREPARATION - Identify energy sources - Type and magnitude of energy - Hazards present - Method of control - Notify affected employees 2. SHUTDOWN - Orderly shutdown using normal procedures - No damage to equipment - Notify operators 3. ISOLATION - Disconnect energy sources - Open circuit breakers/disconnects - Close valves - Block moving parts - Position to "off" or "safe" 4. Summary

  • Current kills, not voltage; as little as 100 mA can cause ventricular fibrillation
  • NEC (NFPA 70) establishes minimum safety requirements for electrical installations
  • GFCI protection required in wet locations, trips at 5 mA in 0.025 seconds
  • Grounding limits voltages; bonding provides fault current path (primary protection)
  • Arc flash hazards produce 35,000Â°F temperatures; proper PPE is mandatory
  • Lockout/Tagout prevents unexpected energization; test before touch always
  • NFPA 70E defines arc flash boundaries and PPE categories
  • IEEE standards cover design (142, 242), safety (80, 1584), and power quality (519)
  • IEC standards used internationally; IP ratings define ingress protection
  • Qualified workers only for energized work; training saves lives
