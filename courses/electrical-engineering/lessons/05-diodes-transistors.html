Introduction to Transistors

Transistors are the fundamental building blocks of modern electronics, enabling amplification, switching, and signal processing. They revolutionized electronics by replacing vacuum tubes with solid-state devices that are smaller, more reliable, and consume less power.

Summary

  • BJT is current-controlled device (I_C = Î² Ã— I_B)
  • BJT regions: Cutoff (OFF), Active (amplifier), Saturation (switch ON)
  • Voltage divider bias provides best stability for BJT amplifiers
  • Three BJT configurations: CE (gain), CC (buffer), CB (high freq)
  • MOSFET is voltage-controlled device (gate insulated, very high input Z)
  • MOSFET regions: Cutoff (V_GS < V_th), Triode, Saturation
  • Enhancement MOSFET normally OFF, requires V_GS > V_th to conduct
  • MOSFETs excel as switches due to low R_DS(on) and fast switching
  • BJTs better for linear amplifiers, MOSFETs better for switching and power
