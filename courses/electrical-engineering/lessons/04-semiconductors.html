Introduction to Semiconductors

Semiconductors revolutionized electronics and enabled the digital age. These materials have electrical conductivity between conductors and insulators, and their conductivity can be precisely controlled through doping and external influences.

Summary

  • Semiconductors have conductivity between conductors and insulators
  • Silicon is the dominant semiconductor material (band gap 1.12 eV)
  • Intrinsic semiconductors are pure, with n = p = n_i
  • N-type doping (Group V) adds free electrons (majority carriers)
  • P-type doping (Group III) adds holes (majority carriers)
  • PN junction creates depletion region with built-in potential (~0.7V for Si)
  • Forward bias allows current flow, reverse bias blocks current
  • Diode I-V relationship is exponential (Shockley equation)
  • Special diodes: Zener (regulation), LED (light), Schottky (fast), Photodiode (sensor)
