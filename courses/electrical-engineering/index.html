Welcome to Electrical Engineering!

Electrical Engineering is the branch of engineering that deals with the study and application of electricity, electronics, and electromagnetism. This course will take you from fundamental concepts to advanced topics used in modern electrical systems design.

What You'll Learn

  • Circuit Analysis - Ohm's Law, Kirchhoff's Laws, mesh and nodal analysis, Thevenin & Norton theorems
  • DC & AC Circuits - Resistive, capacitive, and inductive circuits, impedance, phasor analysis
  • Semiconductor Devices - Diodes, transistors, MOSFETs, device characteristics and applications
  • Operational Amplifiers - Op-amp circuits, inverting/non-inverting configurations, filters
  • Digital Electronics - Logic gates, Boolean algebra, combinational and sequential circuits
  • Power Systems - Three-phase systems, power factor correction, distribution networks
  • Machines - Transformers, AC/DC motors, generators, motor control
  • Control Systems - Transfer functions, stability analysis, PID controllers, feedback systems
  • Signals & Systems - Fourier analysis, Laplace transforms, frequency response
  • Safety & Standards - Electrical safety, NEC codes, grounding, protection devices

üìå Prerequisites

To get the most from this course, you should have:

  • Basic mathematics (algebra, trigonometry)
  • Understanding of calculus (derivatives, integrals) - helpful but not required initially

Learning Approach

Each lesson follows this structure to maximize learning:

  1. Theory & Principles - Mathematical foundations and physical concepts
  2. Worked Examples - Step-by-step problem solving
  3. Circuit Diagrams - Visual representation of concepts
  4. Practical Applications - Real-world use cases
  5. Simulation Exercises - Hands-on practice with circuit simulators
  6. Practice Problems - Reinforce your understanding

üéØ Study Tips

  • Work through every example - Don't just read, calculate!
  • Draw circuit diagrams - Visual understanding is crucial
  • Use simulation software - Verify your calculations
  • Build physical circuits - Theory + practice = mastery
  • Review mathematics - Complex numbers, trigonometry, calculus
  • Keep a reference notebook - Write down key formulas and concepts

Sample Projects You'll Understand

  • Design and analyze voltage divider circuits
  • Build amplifier circuits using transistors and op-amps
  • Create digital logic circuits for practical applications
  • Analyze three-phase power distribution systems
  • Design filter circuits (low-pass, high-pass, band-pass)
  • Implement PID controllers for motor control
  • Calculate power requirements for residential/industrial installations
  • Design rectifier and power supply circuits
